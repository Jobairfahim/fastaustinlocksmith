How Deadbolt’s Work

Latches are the more common locking mechanism for doors but, in areas that require tougher security, they are often accompanied by a deadbolt, a thicker and heavier locking mechanism that only locks and unlocks with a key. While latches can typically be unlocked by turning the knob from the inside, deadbolts require manual engagement. Plus, spring activated latches can be jimmied open with a card or knife blade. Latches are useful if you are around the area, but deadbolts provide the tough security that you need when you are away from the property for an extended time.

Some Types of Deadbolt Locks

The fundamental mechanism of a deadbolt lock is always the same, but there are different types of deadbolt locks that each have their benefits and drawbacks.

  • A single cylinder deadbolt is manually unlocked with a thumbpiece on the interior of the door and with a key on the exterior side. These are not very useful if there is glass within 30 inches of the thumbpiece though as a criminal could break the glass and reach in.
  • A double cylinder deadbolt requires a key on both sides of the door. Though they provide security even if there is glass around, double cylinder deadbolts can be a safety hazard as it blocks escape to those without a key.

There are also modern versions that use keypads or access cards rather than a key to enter. Call Fast Austin Locksmith to learn about your options and choose the best one for your property.
